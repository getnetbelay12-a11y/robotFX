# CareProof Next 30-Day Plan After Day 90

## Decision

- [ ] Continue
- [ ] Scale
- [x] Pivot
- [ ] Pause

## Main Goal

Validate a narrower wedge and a harder execution model: can a family-update-focused offer create real buyer conversations and at least one pilot setup discussion when contact volume is actually logged?

## Primary Segment

Private-pay non-medical home care agencies with small admin teams, manual family update workflows, and no immediate EVV or payroll requirement.

## Primary Message

After a caregiver visit, how does the family know what was completed?

CareProof should be positioned first as a simple family update and proof-of-care workflow, not as a broad home care operations platform.

## Top 3 Metrics

1. Calls made and logged
2. Conversations started
3. Pilot setup discussions created

## Product Focus

1. Keep the family update workflow as the lead wedge
2. Keep caregiver checklist and note flow simple enough for a small pilot
3. Avoid adding platform complexity until real willingness to pay appears

## Sales / Customer Success Focus

1. Use call-first outreach with same-day logging as the non-negotiable default
2. Close or deprioritize silent rows quickly instead of carrying false pipeline weight
3. Ask for 10-minute discovery or pilot-setup conversations, not broad demos first

## Week 1

Build a fresh 20-agency list in the narrow segment, execute phone-first outreach, and log every contact outcome inside the workspace the same day.

## Week 2

Double down only on agencies or channels that create real replies or conversations; kill the dead segment quickly.

## Week 3

Push every live conversation toward one of two outcomes: a 10-minute discovery call or a small 14-day pilot setup discussion.

## Week 4

Review whether the narrower wedge produced real buyer signal, pilot interest, or willingness to pay. If not, prepare to pause product work and shift to deeper customer discovery.

## Do Not Build

- Billing
- Payroll
- EVV
- AI diagnosis
- Custom branding
- Advanced dashboards
- Chat/video
- Marketplace

## Success Criteria

- At least 20 real outreach contacts are logged
- At least 3 real buyer conversations are created
- At least 1 pilot setup discussion is opened
- Silent opportunities are closed quickly instead of carried forward

## Failure Criteria

- Outreach is still not logged reliably
- No real buyer conversations appear
- The wedge still does not create pilot interest
- The team starts expanding product scope before buyer signal improves
