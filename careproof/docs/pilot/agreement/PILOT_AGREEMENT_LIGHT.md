# CareProof 30-Day Pilot Agreement

This pilot agreement outlines the intended scope for a limited CareProof pilot.

## Purpose

The purpose of the pilot is to evaluate whether CareProof helps the agency improve caregiver documentation, family updates, proof-of-care visibility, and care communication.

## Pilot Duration

The pilot will run for 30 days unless extended by mutual agreement.

## Pilot Scope

The pilot includes:

- Admin dashboard
- Caregiver mobile workflow
- Visit check-in/check-out
- Care checklist
- Care notes
- Incident reporting
- Family updates
- Family concern submission
- Weekly reports

## Not Included

The pilot does not include:

- Payroll
- Billing
- Medicaid EVV replacement
- EHR integration
- Medical diagnosis
- Emergency response service
- Insurance claims processing

## Agency Responsibilities

The agency is responsible for:

- Selecting pilot caregivers and clients
- Getting any required consent from caregivers, clients, and family members
- Reviewing family-facing communication policies
- Assigning a pilot champion
- Attending weekly review sessions
- Reporting issues promptly

## CareProof Responsibilities

CareProof will provide:

- Pilot setup support
- Basic training
- Data import support
- Weekly review support
- Access to the pilot environment
- Issue tracking during the pilot

## Important Disclaimer

CareProof is an operational communication and proof-of-care tool. It is not an emergency response service, medical device, clinical diagnosis tool, payroll system, billing system, or formal EVV replacement.

For emergencies, users should call 911 or local emergency services.

## Data

At the end of the pilot, the agency may request export of key pilot records. CareProof will not use agency-identifiable data publicly without permission.

## Continuation

If the pilot is successful, the agency may continue on a paid plan. Starter pricing begins at $149/month.

This is not a substitute for legal counsel, but it is better than running a pilot with no written scope.
