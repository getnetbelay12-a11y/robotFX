# Day 19 Adoption Runbook

## Goal

Make CareProof part of the agency's daily routine.

The pilot can fail even when bugs are fixed if caregivers forget steps, admins do not follow up, or families do not understand the update process.

## Start With Adoption Metrics

Pull the last 2 to 3 days of pilot data and track:

- Scheduled pilot visits
- Visits started in CareProof
- Visits completed in CareProof
- Checklist completion rate
- Care note completion rate
- Family updates generated
- Late visits detected
- Missed visits detected
- Caregivers who used the app
- Caregivers who did not use the app

## Low-Usage Caregiver Signals

Identify caregivers who:

- Had scheduled visits but did not check in
- Checked in but did not complete checklist
- Completed checklist but skipped care note
- Started visit but did not check out
- Repeatedly needed admin help

## Diagnostic Questions

Ask:

1. Did they forget?
2. Could they not log in?
3. Was the visit missing?
4. Was the app confusing?
5. Was the checklist too long?
6. Was internet or GPS the issue?
7. Did they understand why the agency wants this?

Do not blame caregivers too fast. Diagnose the friction.

## If Adoption Is Poor

If fewer than 50 percent of visits are started in CareProof:

1. Reduce the pilot group to the best 2 to 3 caregivers
2. Confirm visit assignments are correct
3. Re-train with a 10-minute session
4. Simplify the checklist
5. Make the champion monitor every visit
6. Relaunch the controlled group tomorrow

## If Adoption Is Strong

If caregivers are using it and families are receiving updates, move toward the first weekly review preparation next.
