# Day 23 Family Communication Runbook

## Goal

Determine whether family updates are useful, clear, safe, and reducing manual calls or texts.

If family updates are weak, CareProof becomes just another internal checklist tool.

## Start With Update Performance

Pull all completed visits from Week 2 so far and check:

- Completed visits
- Family updates generated
- Family updates sent
- Family update failures
- Family updates viewed or opened if tracked
- Family concerns submitted
- Agency responses to concerns

## Safety Review

For every family update sampled, verify:

- Correct client
- Correct family member
- No internal admin notes
- No audit trail
- No caregiver private phone or email
- No raw high-severity incident detail
- No scary or diagnostic medical wording

## Clarity Review

A family update should answer:

1. Was the visit completed
2. What tasks were done
3. Was there anything the agency will follow up on
4. What can the family do if they have a concern

## Agency Value Questions

Ask the pilot champion:

- Did families call less this week
- Did any family mention the CareProof update
- Were any family members confused
- Did staff still need to manually text families
- Did any family submit a concern through CareProof
- Were concerns easier to track than phone or text

The strongest signal is:

We did not have to call or text families as much.
