# Day 27 Conversion Meeting Runbook

## Goal

Turn pilot feedback into a business decision.

The outcome must be one of these:

1. Continue paid
2. Extend pilot with clear success criteria
3. Stop because it is not a fit

Do not accept vague “let’s keep discussing.”

## 30-Minute Structure

### 0-3 Minutes: Restate Original Pilot Goal

Say:

The purpose of today’s meeting is to decide whether CareProof should continue after the pilot.

We will review the pilot results, what value your team saw, what gaps remain, and then decide one of three paths: continue paid, extend with clear success criteria, or stop if it is not the right fit.

### 3-8 Minutes: Show Usage Results

Review:

- Scheduled pilot visits
- Visits completed through CareProof
- Checklist completion
- Care note completion
- Family updates generated
- Late and missed visits
- Incidents and family concerns

### 8-13 Minutes: Show Value Proof

Use the strongest pilot evidence:

- family update visibility
- caregiver documentation
- proof timeline
- operational issue detection

### 13-18 Minutes: Discuss Remaining Gaps

Be explicit:

- adoption gaps
- workflow gaps
- feature blockers
- pricing hesitation

### 18-23 Minutes: Present Continuation Plan

Say:

Based on the pilot results, my recommendation is to continue CareProof for one paid month on the Starter plan.

The Starter plan is $149/month and includes:

- up to 15 caregivers
- admin dashboard
- caregiver mobile workflow
- family updates
- incident and concern tracking
- weekly reports
- basic support

### 23-27 Minutes: Ask For Decision

Ask clearly:

Would you like to continue CareProof after the pilot on the Starter plan?

Then stop talking.

### 27-30 Minutes: Confirm Next Steps

If yes:

- confirm billing contact
- confirm start date
- confirm active scope

If maybe:

- define 7-day extension criteria

If no:

- ask for the main reason and document it
