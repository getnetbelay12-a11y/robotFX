## First Market Signals

These signals are inferred from public agency positioning, not from live conversations yet:

- Small and mid-size agencies repeatedly lead with trust language such as family peace of mind, compassionate caregivers, and dependable support.
- Many agencies mention companionship, medication reminders, respite care, and dementia support, but very few visibly describe a structured family update workflow.
- Private-duty and companion-care agencies are more common in the target list than agencies openly discussing operational accountability tools.
- Franchise operators appear often in search results, but they are weaker first-pilot targets because they likely have more process overhead and less flexibility.

## Common Pain Signals Observed

- Family reassurance is a visible marketing message.
- Companion care and personal care are common service lines.
- Medication reminders appear often, which suggests visit proof and task documentation matter operationally.
- Respite and dementia care are frequently mentioned, which usually increases family communication pressure.

## Common Objections Expected

- We already have software.
- Families will not use another app.
- Caregivers are not technical.
- Is this HIPAA compliant.

## Best Responses

- CareProof is not trying to replace scheduling or billing software.
- The first value can come through email or SMS updates before a family portal matters.
- The caregiver workflow is intentionally short: start visit, checklist, note, end visit.
- Use privacy-safe wording only: role-based access, audit logs, secure authentication, and agency-level data isolation.

## Weakness In Our Current Execution

- The list is now built, but outreach has not actually happened yet.
- Too many rows still rely on front-desk contacts instead of named owners or coordinators.
- Several agencies still need direct email verification before they move into the first 20-send batch.
- No live objections have been collected yet, so the message is still unproven.
