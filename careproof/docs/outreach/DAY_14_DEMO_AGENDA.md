# Day 14 Demo Agenda

## Goal

The goal of the demo is not to show every screen. The goal is to prove one thing:

CareProof can reduce family update calls, improve caregiver accountability, and give the agency a clearer proof-of-care record.

## 20-Minute Structure

### 0-3 Minutes: Current Process

Ask:

1. How do families currently know a caregiver completed a visit?
2. How often do families call asking for updates?
3. How do caregivers document what they did today?
4. What happens when a caregiver is late or misses a visit?
5. What software do you use now?

### 3-5 Minutes: Position CareProof

Say:

CareProof is not trying to replace your scheduling or billing software.

It focuses on the trust gap after a visit starts:
caregiver proof, family updates, incident visibility, and weekly reports.

### 5-13 Minutes: Product Demo

Show in this order:

1. Agency dashboard
2. Visit detail proof timeline
3. Caregiver workflow
4. Family update
5. Incident safety
6. Weekly report

### 13-16 Minutes: Pilot Offer

Say:

The best way to test this is a controlled 30-day pilot.

We would start small:

- 5 to 10 caregivers
- 5 to 20 clients
- family updates enabled
- weekly review
- clear success metrics

### 16-19 Minutes: Questions and Objections

Listen for:

- existing software
- caregiver adoption risk
- family adoption risk
- compliance concerns
- EVV requirements
- pricing signals

### 19-20 Minutes: Close

Ask:

Would you be open to testing this with a small group of caregivers and families?

If yes or maybe:

Would Tuesday or Thursday work better for a pilot setup call?

## Demo Angle Selection

If they complain about family calls:

Focus on the family update and weekly report.

If they complain about caregiver documentation:

Focus on check-in, checklist, note, and checkout.

If they complain about late or missed visits:

Focus on the dashboard urgent queue and visit status visibility.
